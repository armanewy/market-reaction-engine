Summary • In other systemic amyloidoses, the residual, absolute
concentration of the amyloid precursor protein is closely associated with clinical outcomes • Interim data from 62 patients with ATTR amyloidosis treated with NTLA-2001 continue to show a favorable safety and tolerability profile, with rapid,
consistent, and durable reductions of serum [TTR] to low levels in all patients • For treatments that reduce total serum [TTR], with nonfluctuating steady state measures, the residual absolute serum [TTR] could be a robust biomarker of ATTR
amyloidosis therapy outcomes • With collaboration, this approach to biomarker development has facilitated progress in care and better outcomes in AA and AL amyloidosis AA, amyloid A; AL, amyloid light chain; ATTR, transthyretin amyloidosis;
TTR, transthyretin.
