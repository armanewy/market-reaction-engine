Item 1.01

Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement.

Agreement and Plan of Merger

On May 16, 2025, BioMarin Pharmaceutical Inc. (BioMarin) entered into an Agreement and Plan of Merger (the Merger Agreement) with Incline Merger Sub, Inc., a Delaware corporation and a wholly-owned subsidiary of BioMarin (Merger Sub), and Inozyme Pharma, Inc., a Delaware corporation (Inozyme).

Pursuant to the Merger Agreement, and upon the terms and subject to the conditions therein, Merger Sub will commence a cash tender offer (the Offer) no later than June 2, 2025, to purchase all of the outstanding shares of the common stock, par value $0.0001 per share, of Inozyme (the Shares) at a price of $4.00 per Share, without interest and subject to any applicable withholding taxes (the Offer Price). The Offer will remain open for 20 business days, subject to extension under certain circumstances.

The obligation of Merger Sub to accept for payment and pay for Shares validly tendered (and not validly withdrawn) pursuant to the Offer is subject to the satisfaction or waiver of a number of conditions set forth in the Merger Agreement, including (a) that the number of Shares (i) validly tendered (and not validly withdrawn) prior to the time that the Offer expires, and (ii) otherwise beneficially owned by BioMarin or Merger Sub (or any wholly-owned subsidiary of BioMarin or Merger Sub), if any, collectively represent at least one (1) Share more than 50% of the then-issued and outstanding Shares as of the expiration of the Offer (excluding any Shares tendered pursuant to guaranteed delivery procedures that have not yet been “received” (as such term is defined in Section 251(h)(6)(f) of the Delaware General Corporation Law (the DGCL))); (b) the accuracy of the representations and warranties of Inozyme contained in the Merger Agreement, subject to customary thresholds and exceptions as set forth therein; (c) Inozyme’s compliance with and performance in all material respects of the covenants and agreements it is required to comply with or perform at or prior to the Offer Acceptance Time (as defined in the Merger Agreement); (d) the absence of a Material Adverse Effect (as defined in the Merger Agreement) that is continuing as of the Offer Acceptance Time; (e) the expiration or termination of the waiting period (or any extension thereof) applicable to the Offer under the Hart-Scott-Rodino Antitrust Improvements Act of 1976, as amended, and of any timing agreement with any Governmental Body (as defined in the Merger Agreement) and (f) other customary conditions set forth in Annex I to the Merger Agreement.

Following the consummation of the Offer, upon the terms and subject to the conditions set forth in the Merger Agreement and in accordance with the DGCL, Merger Sub will merge with and into Inozyme, with Inozyme as the surviving corporation and will become a wholly-owned subsidiary of BioMarin (the Merger). At the effective time of the Merger (the Effective Time), by virtue of the Merger and without any further action on the part of BioMarin, Merger Sub, Inozyme or any other stockholder of Inozyme, each Share (other than (a) Shares held by Inozyme (including Shares held in Inozyme’s treasury) as of immediately prior to the Effective Time, (b) Shares held by BioMarin, Merger Sub or any other direct or indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of BioMarin as of immediately prior to the Effective Time, (c) Shares held by stockholders of Inozyme who have properly exercised and perfected their statutory rights of appraisal under the DGCL and (d) Shares irrevocably accepted for purchase in the Offer) will be converted into the right to receive an amount equal to the Offer Price, without any interest thereon and subject to any withholding of applicable taxes.

The Merger Agreement includes customary representations, warranties and covenants of BioMarin, Merger Sub and Inozyme for a transaction of this nature, including covenants regarding the operation of Inozyme’s business prior to the Effective Time.

Inozyme has agreed to customary restrictions on its ability to solicit alternative acquisition proposals from third parties and engage in discussions or negotiations with third parties regarding acquisition proposals. Notwithstanding these restrictions, Inozyme may under certain circumstances provide information to and participate in discussions or negotiations with third parties with respect to an unsolicited bona fide written acquisition proposal that the board of directors of Inozyme determines in good faith, after consultation with its financial advisor and outside legal counsel, constitutes or could

reasonably be expected to lead to a Superior Offer (as defined in the Merger Agreement), and the failure to take such action would be inconsistent with Inozyme’s board of directors’ fiduciary duties under applicable law.

The Merger Agreement also provides that, in connection with the termination of the Merger Agreement under specified circumstances, including termination by Inozyme to accept and enter into an agreement with respect to a Superior Offer, Inozyme will be required to pay BioMarin a termination fee in the amount of $8,700,000 in cash.

The board of directors of Inozyme has unanimously (a) determined that the Merger Agreement and the transactions contemplated by the Merger Agreement, including the Offer and the Merger (collectively, the Transactions), are advisable, fair to, and in the best interest of, Inozyme and its stockholders, (b) approved the execution, delivery and performance by Inozyme of the Merger Agreement and the consummation by Inozyme of the Transactions on the terms and subject to the conditions set forth in the Merger Agreement, (c) resolved that the Merger shall be effected under Section 251(h) of the DGCL and (d) resolved to recommend that Inozyme stockholders tender their Shares to Merger Sub pursuant to the Offer.

Tender and Support Agreements

In connection and concurrently with the execution of the Merger Agreement, BioMarin entered into tender and support agreements (the Support Agreements) with certain of Inozyme’s stockholders. The Support Agreements provide that, among other things, those parties irrevocably tender the Shares held by them in the Offer, upon the terms and subject to the conditions of such Support Agreements. As of May 16, 2025, the Shares subject to the Support Agreements comprise an aggregate of approximately 11.2% of the outstanding Shares. The Support Agreements will terminate upon termination of the Merger Agreement and certain other specified events.

The form of the Support Agreement is attached as Exhibit 10.1 and is incorporated herein by reference. The foregoing description of the Support Agreements does not purport to be complete and is qualified in its entirety by reference to the full text thereof.
